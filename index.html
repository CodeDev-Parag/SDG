The Problem

The Awareness Gap that Costs Futures

India has one of the world's largest ecosystems of free digital learning and government-backed education schemes — yet millions never benefit from them, simply because they don't know these resources exist.

  • ◆Only 1 in 5 rural students knows about SWAYAM or DIKSHA
  • ◆Only 26% of eligible scholarship beneficiaries actually apply
  • ◆Over 3 crore children remain out of school (UNESCO, 2023)
  • ◆Students miss scholarship deadlines — no one informed them in time
  • ◆Language barriers and lack of digital literacy widen the divide

India's Education Journey

Milestones that Shaped Learning

1947

Independence & 12% Literacy

India starts its journey with a 12% literacy rate. Education for all becomes a foundational mission.

2009

Right to Education Act

Free and compulsory education becomes a fundamental right for all children aged 6–14 years.

2017

SWAYAM Platform Launch

Government launches SWAYAM with thousands of free online courses from premier institutions.

2020

National Education Policy

NEP 2020 — transformative policy to modernize education, promote multilingualism and digital learning.

2023

77.7% Literacy Achieved

India crosses 77.7% literacy — a testament to decades of persistent educational reforms and investment.

Our Mission

Why VidhyaDhan Exists

VidhyaDhan means "Wealth of Knowledge" — because we believe education is the most valuable resource any person can possess.

This platform is a centralized awareness hub — not another education portal. We don't create courses; we make sure every eligible student knows exactly which free resources they can access, which schemes they qualify for, and which scholarships have open deadlines.
