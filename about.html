Our Story

About VidhyaDhan

We are not another education platform. We are a bridge — connecting every deserving Indian student to the free resources that are rightfully theirs.

Our Mission

Awareness is the First Step to Education

India has built one of the world's most ambitious educational ecosystems — SWAYAM, DIKSHA, NPTEL, the National Scholarship Portal, PM KVY, and dozens more. Yet millions of students who need these resources the most have never heard of them.

"The greatest injustice is not the absence of opportunity — it is the unawareness of it."

VidhyaDhan (विद्याधन) — meaning Wealth of Knowledge — was created to solve this single, devastating problem. We aggregate information about every major free education platform, government scheme, and scholarship portal in one place, presented in a clear, accessible, and beautiful format.

This is a zero-cost, zero-profit awareness initiative aligned with India's commitment to SDG Goal 4: Quality Education for All.

Questions & Answers

Frequently Asked Questions

Is VidhyaDhan affiliated with the Government of India?

+
VidhyaDhan is an independent awareness initiative and is not officially affiliated with the Government of India. However, all information listed on this platform is sourced directly from official government portals (swayam.gov.in, diksha.gov.in, scholarships.gov.in, etc.) and is updated regularly for accuracy.

Are the courses and scholarships really free?

+
Yes. Every platform and scheme listed on VidhyaDhan offers free access to learning content. Some platforms (like SWAYAM and NPTEL) offer optional paid certification exams, but the course content itself is completely free. Scholarships listed are real government schemes with verified disbursements.

Do I need a smartphone or computer to access these platforms?

+
Not necessarily. PM e-VIDYA offers education through 34 DTH TV channels and radio — no internet or device required. DIKSHA has an offline mode. For students with smartphones, most platforms have dedicated apps with low-data or offline modes. We recommend DIKSHA and SWAYAM apps for mobile learners.

How do I apply for multiple scholarships at once?

+
The National Scholarship Portal (scholarships.gov.in) is a single-window system. After registering once with your Aadhaar, you can apply to all eligible central government scholarships simultaneously. The system automatically matches you with schemes based on your profile. There is no limit on how many schemes you can apply to.

What documents are typically required for scholarships?

+
Most scholarships require: Aadhaar Card, Class 10/12 marksheets, Bank account details (in student's name), Income certificate from a government authority, Caste certificate (if applicable), Admission letter or fee receipt from the institution, and a recent passport-size photograph. Prepare these documents before the deadline to avoid last-minute issues.

Can I share VidhyaDhan with students in my village or community?

+
Absolutely — and we encourage it! VidhyaDhan was built precisely for this purpose. Please share this website with teachers, parents, NGOs, gram panchayats, and any student who could benefit. Print pages if needed, share links, or simply walk through it with someone who needs it. Every share potentially unlocks a future.
